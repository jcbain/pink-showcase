Oh to Begin Again

by James Bain

Tuesday Nov 26, 2019

151 Words

It was neaaring the 11 o’clock hour and Jennifer and I had just made it to the border crossing at Sweet Grass, Montana. I admit, I felt a bit guily emigrating to Canada on the Fourth of July but that feeling quickly subsided as my attention turned to the border agent as he told us that we filled out our importation forms incorrectly. An hour and a half later we were making our way up to Calgary min

For three years, we had been wanting to move to the mountains, wherever that meant. For a year and half of those three years, I had been actively rummaging through job opportunities that could take us there despite it not being “the right time”. I was finishing a PhD, which was simulataneously the reason why we couldn’t leave along with the reason for which I very much eager to.

I am a shameful-self-promoter.
